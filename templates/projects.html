My Projects

Photography

During my freshman year of college, my friend bought a $700 DSLR camera for a photography class he was taking. At the time, I didn’t know much about photography, and thought it was a waste of money to buy such an expensive device just for taking photos when I could use my smartphone. Sure, the quality of his digital camera was far better than the pictures on my phone, but in the end, pictures were pictures, right? That’s what I thought while I watched my friend walking around with his camera trying to look artsy. That’s what I thought before one boring evening when I decided to pick up his camera and snap a few shots of my friend. I followed a few people on Instagram who would edit their photos to make them look more aesthetic or surreal, and thought I would try that with my friend’s face. Using Photoshop, I messed around with different edits and instantly fell in love. I was intrigued by how much better a raw photo could look with a few edits and also began to realize how creative I could get with a photograph. From then on, I taught myself how to use Photoshop and used my friend’s camera to take pictures whenever I could. I now own a NikonD5600.

Digital Art

My passion for digital art began 6 years ago, when I started learning Adobe Illustrator back in New Jersey. I enjoyed sketching as a kid and liked to design interesting creatures or monsters. Doing something similar on a digital screen felt even easier and less time consuming. Creating digital art came natural to me and I liked being able create anything that came to mind. I created visual representations of ideas or issues around the world. I recreated characters from videogames I enjoyed playing such as League of Legends. I created anything interesting that came to mind really. I think that Illustrator is an amazing tool to know because it allows me to visualize anything and easily create a digital replica. I also use digital art to design interfaces for websites or create flyers, banners, and logos.

Banner Design

During my freshman year, I joined the Society of Asian Scientists and Engineers (SASE), an organization founded in 2007 to help Asian heritage scientific and engineering professionals achieve their potential and success. While I was attracted to being part of a group that celebrated diversity on campus and provided academic opportunities for its members, I wanted to contribute further and introduce more people with these opportunities. So the next year, I ran for the executive board and was elected as the Publicity Chair of the University of Rochester (UR) SASE. As the Publicity Chair, I am responsible for all of UR SASE’s advertisements, promotions, etc., both digital and physical, as well as marketing techniques and the promotion of SASE branding. I manage SASE’s social media sites such as Instagram and Facebook and create flyers for different events to put around campus. I also design and create banners for the events that I post on UR SASE’s Facebook page.
